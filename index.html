Clemencia Siro

PhD Candidate, University of Amsterdam

About me ...

I am a third year PhD candidate at the University of Amsterdam, supervised by Maarten de Rijke and Mohammad Alian Nejadi. I completed my MSc degree from African Institute for Mathematical Sciences - AMMI (Rwanda) supervised by Siva Reddy from McGill University. I received my BSc from Jomo Kenyatta Uniersity, Kenya. My research focus is on the evaluation of conversational systems, specifically from the user's perspective. I aim to improve user experience in their interaction with AI systems. I am also interested on how to leverage LLMs for evaluation and studying how factors affecting humans in system evaluation, affect LLMs when used as evaluators. Apart from that I am an active member of Masakhane where I take part in promoting NLP for Low-resource African languages.

Research interests:

  • User-centric evaluation of conversational systems
  • Evaluation of and with large language models
  • NLP for low-resource African languages
